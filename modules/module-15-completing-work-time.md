# Module 15: Completing Work in Time

**Duration:** ~25 mins | **Topics:** 1 | **Level:** Beginner

## Overview
Implement Service Level Agreements (SLAs) to ensure work is completed within defined timeframes. SLAs track case progress and alert stakeholders when deadlines approach.

## Learning Objectives
- Configure Service Level Agreements (SLAs) for cases.
- Set deadline targets for case processing.
- Configure alerts and escalations for SLA violations.

## Topic 1: Service Level Agreements
### Overview
SLAs define target timeframes for completing cases or specific steps. The system tracks progress against these targets and alerts stakeholders when cases approach or exceed deadlines.

### Key Concepts
- **SLA:** Service Level Agreement defining timeframe
- **Deadline:** Target completion time
- **Monitoring:** Track progress against SLA
- **Alerts:** Notify when approaching deadline
- **Escalation:** Action when deadline exceeded
- **Business hours:** Option to count only business time
- **Priority-based:** Different SLAs for different cases

### How It Works
Developers configure SLAs for cases or workflow steps with target timeframes. The system monitors progress and alerts users when cases approach deadlines. Escalation rules trigger actions when deadlines are missed, such as notifying managers or automatically escalating to higher priority users.

### Takeaways
- SLAs enforce timeframe compliance
- Monitoring prevents cases from exceeding targets
- Alerts provide early warning of approaching deadlines
- Escalation ensures action on overdue items
- Business hour options accommodate service hour variations

## Module Summary
SLAs are essential for maintaining service quality and meeting business commitments. By defining target timeframes and configuring monitoring and escalation, organizations ensure timely case completion and proactive issue management.

## CSA Exam Tips
1. Understand that SLAs define target completion timeframes
2. Know that the system monitors progress and alerts on approaching deadlines
3. Remember that escalation rules handle SLA violations
4. Understand business hour options for SLA calculations
5. Be prepared to explain how SLAs support service quality management
