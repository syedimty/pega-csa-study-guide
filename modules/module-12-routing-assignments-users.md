# Module 12: Routing Assignments to Users

**Duration:** ~25 mins | **Topics:** 1 | **Level:** Beginner

## Overview
Assign responsibility for completing a Task to one or more users. Routing increases business efficiency by ensuring that the most appropriate users complete specific Assignments.

## Learning Objectives
- Identify the value of assigning and routing Tasks to users.
- Differentiate between a Worklist and a Work Queue.
- Route Tasks to one or more users.

## Topic 1: Routing Work
### Overview
Routing is the process of directing work (Tasks) to appropriate users or teams. Effective routing ensures work reaches the most qualified or available resources, improving case completion efficiency and business outcomes.

### Key Concepts
- **Routing:** Process of assigning work to users or teams
- **Worklist:** Individual user's queue of assigned work
- **Work Queue:** Shared queue of work for a team
- **Assignment:** Specific task assigned to a user
- **Routing rules:** Logic determining who receives work
- **Load balancing:** Distribute work among available users
- **Skill-based routing:** Route based on user capabilities

### How It Works
Developers define routing logic that determines which users or queues receive assignments. Work can be routed to individual users (appearing in their Worklist) or to shared queues (Work Queues) where available users can pick up work. Routing can be based on user skills, availability, or other business criteria.

### Takeaways
- Routing directs work to appropriate resources
- Worklists are individual user queues
- Work Queues are shared team queues
- Routing rules can be based on skills, availability, or other criteria
- Effective routing improves efficiency and case completion

## Module Summary
Routing is fundamental to case processing efficiency. By directing work to appropriate users or teams through configured routing rules, organizations ensure that work reaches the most qualified resources. The distinction between individual Worklists and shared Work Queues provides flexibility in work distribution strategies.

## CSA Exam Tips
1. Understand that routing assigns Tasks to users or teams based on configured rules
2. Know the distinction: Worklist (individual) vs Work Queue (shared team queue)
3. Remember that routing can be based on skills, availability, or other business logic
4. Understand that effective routing improves case completion and business efficiency
5. Be prepared to explain skill-based routing and load balancing concepts
